# Gaz Express Doctrine

Status: Draft
Project: Gaz Express Conversion OS
Date: 2026-06-21

## 1. Purpose

Gaz Express Conversion OS exists to convert cold restaurant and hospitality prospects into LPG customers by building trust, reducing supplier-risk anxiety, and starting a WhatsApp conversation.

The system must support a field representative who has already popped in or cold-called and collected the owner or manager's contact details.

## 2. Strategic Positioning

Gaz Express must not be positioned as a generic LPG supplier.

Gaz Express must be positioned as a kitchen continuity partner.

Restaurants do not only buy LPG. They buy:

- no kitchen downtime
- reliable supply
- fast response
- clear pricing
- one less supplier problem
- confidence during service hours

## 3. Primary User

The primary user is a hospitality decision maker.

Examples:

- restaurant owner
- chef-owner
- hotel manager
- guesthouse owner
- takeaway operator
- operations manager
- procurement decision maker

## 4. Core User Fear

The strongest emotional trigger is running out of gas during service.

Every major asset must answer the question:

Can I trust these people to keep my kitchen running?

## 5. Conversion Model

The system is a conversion loop, not a static landing page.

Representative visit
-> WhatsApp introduction
-> mobile landing page
-> trust building
-> WhatsApp conversation
-> sales follow-up
-> quote
-> customer

## 6. Channel Doctrine

WhatsApp is the primary conversion channel.

Forms are secondary.

The goal of the landing page is not to collect a passive lead. The goal is to open a real conversation with the sales team.

## 7. Asset Hierarchy

### Acquisition Layer

Field representative visit and first WhatsApp message.

### Trust Layer

Mobile-first landing page that builds confidence.

### Proof Layer

Company profile PDF, customer proof, testimonials, delivery coverage, safety commitment and real operational photographs.

### Conversion Layer

WhatsApp-first quote request.

### Follow-Up Layer

Structured follow-up messages after the first contact.

## 8. Non-Negotiable Rules

1. Do not make cylinders the hero.
2. Do not make trucks the hero.
3. Do not lead with price before trust.
4. Do not use generic corporate copy.
5. Do not force the user into a long form before a conversation.
6. Do not create assets that cannot be used by a sales rep in the field.
7. Do not make claims that cannot be backed by real proof.
8. Do not hide the WhatsApp CTA.

## 9. Required Trust Signals

The system should collect and display real proof where available:

- years in business
- service area
- team photographs
- depot photographs
- delivery capability
- safety process
- existing hospitality clients
- customer testimonials
- Google reviews
- emergency response capability

## 10. Success Definition

A successful campaign does not only produce page visits.

A successful campaign produces:

- WhatsApp conversations
- pricing requests
- quote opportunities
- repeat follow-ups
- converted hospitality accounts

## 11. Doctrine Summary

The landing page creates confidence.

The WhatsApp loop creates conversion.

The follow-up engine creates sales.
